How to read this table
This table shows your labor cost for the month, broken down by position.
Example — Room Attendant Budget hrs: 5,200h What the approved budget planned Actual hrs: 4,980h Regular hours worked OT hrs: 120h Overtime hours (shown separately) Rate: $18.50/hr Base hourly rate (Labor Model) Budget $: $96,200 Budget hrs × rate (5,200 × $18.50) Regular $: $92,130 Regular hours × rate (4,980 × $18.50) OT $: $3,330 OT hours × rate × 1.5 ← paid at time-and-a-half (120 × $18.50 × 1.5) Actual $: $95,460 Regular $ + OT $ (what you actually spent) Variance $: −$740 ✓ Actual $ − Budget $ Negative = under budget (good)
How staffing is calculated
Enter the projected occupancy for each day of the hotel week (Sat–Fri). Each position is staffed from the standards in your Labor Model.
Example — Room Attendant at 326 rooms/day Hours/day: 183h 326 × 56.2% (Labor Model) Staff/day: 23 183 ÷ 8h shift, rounded up Total staff-days: 161 sum of staff across all 7 days (here 23 × 7) FTEs needed: 32.2 total staff-days ÷ 5
What is an FTE? A Full-Time Equivalent is one employee who works a full schedule of 5 days a week. Because the hotel runs 7 days but each person only works 5, you need more people on the roster than you schedule on any single day — the extra cover days off, vacation and call-offs.

So you'd put 23 room attendants on the floor each day, but you need about 32 people hired to cover the whole week.
